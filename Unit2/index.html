Section 1 of 5

Microsoft 365

The cloud platform that hosts the services you will use throughout this course.

The three layers

Productivity Apps — The tools people use to get work done: Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint.

Cloud Services — Infrastructure services that run in Microsoft's data centers: Microsoft Entra ID (identity), Microsoft Intune (endpoint management), Exchange Online (email), SharePoint Online (file sharing).

Security Tools — Microsoft Defender for Endpoint, Conditional Access policies, Microsoft Purview (compliance).

Microsoft 365 is the platform. Entra ID and Intune are services that run inside it — not separate products.

Section 2 of 5

Microsoft Entra ID

Cloud identity — the system that knows who you are and what you are allowed to do.

Four core capabilities

Authentication — Verifies your identity when you sign in. Supports passwords, PIN, biometrics, and multi-factor authentication.

Access Control — Determines what resources you can reach after sign-in: apps, files, email, devices.

Conditional Access — Adds conditions to sign-in: require MFA from outside the office, block non-compliant devices, flag unusual locations.

Single Sign-On (SSO) — One login works across all connected services. Sign in once, use everything.

Entra ID vs on-premises Active Directory

On-premises AD runs on servers you own, requires domain-joined Windows PCs, and needs a VPN for remote access. Entra ID runs in Microsoft's cloud, works on any device from anywhere, and requires no servers to maintain.

Section 3 of 5

Microsoft Intune

Endpoint management at scale — configure, secure, and maintain devices without physical access.

Six things Intune does

Enroll devices — Register Windows, iOS, Android, and Mac devices into Intune management.

Configuration profiles — Push settings (Wi-Fi, VPN, sleep timeout, restrictions) to devices without touching them.

App deployment — Install, update, or remove apps on any enrolled device remotely.

Compliance policies — Define minimum device health requirements. Block non-compliant devices from company resources.

Update rings — Control when Windows updates are delivered to device groups.

Remote actions — Wipe, lock, restart, or retrieve BitLocker keys from enrolled devices remotely.

Section 4 of 5

Cloud-Managed vs Locally-Managed

Two models for managing Windows endpoints. Most organizations are somewhere in between.

Locally-Managed

Configured via Group Policy and local settings. Identity in on-premises AD. Requires corporate network or VPN. IT physically accesses devices.

Cloud-Managed (Intune)

Configured via Intune profiles pushed over internet. Identity in Entra ID. Works from anywhere. IT manages remotely from the Intune portal.

Section 5 of 5

Device Join Types & Autopilot

How Windows devices connect to Entra ID — and how Autopilot automates provisioning.

Three join types

Entra Joined — Connected only to Entra ID. No on-premises domain. Cloud-native model for new devices.

Entra Registered — Lightweight connection. Typically personal or BYOD devices. Limited company management, retains user ownership.

Hybrid Entra Joined — Connected to both on-premises AD and Entra ID. Used during cloud migration. Still receives Group Policy from the domain.

Windows Autopilot

Automates out-of-box setup. Employee receives device, signs in with Microsoft 365 account, and Autopilot Entra-joins it, enrolls it in Intune, and pushes all apps and policies automatically. IT never touches the machine.

Five things to remember

▸  Microsoft 365 is the platform. Entra ID and Intune are services that live inside it.

▸  Entra ID handles identity: authentication, access control, Conditional Access, and SSO.

▸  Intune manages devices: configuration, compliance, apps, updates, and remote actions.

▸  Cloud-managed and locally-managed are two ends of a spectrum. Most orgs are in between.

▸  Autopilot Entra-joins devices automatically. IT never touches the machine physically.
